<div class="textcontainer"> <p class="margin"> </p> <h3 class="title">Week 8: CNC Milling</h3> <h4 class="title">Idea</h4> <p> I cook a lot and because I use a shared kitchen in Currier, I have to move my stuff around every time I cook. I use a lot of grocery bags for this. So for this assignment, I will be making my life easier by making a grocery bag holder. </p> <h4 class="title">Modelling in Fusion</h4> <p> To start I looked up existing designs online and <a href="https://www.etsy.com/listing/1671150317/grocery-bag-carry-handle-1-trip-carry?" style="color: crimson;">found this one</a> on Etsy. I took a screenshot of it and imported it into Fusion 360. See image below. </p> <div style="display: flex; justify-content: center; gap: 20px; max-width: 100%;"> <div style="text-align: center; max-width: 500px;"> <img src="Canvas.png" alt="Screenshot of grocery bag holder design" style="width: 100%; border-radius: 8px;"> <p style="margin-top: 8px; font-size: 14px; color: #c9c9c9;"> Canvas with reference image on Fusion 360 </p> </div> </div> <br> <p> I then used the fit point spline tool to trace the outline of the design to make a 2D design. I made sure to change dimensions of the canvas to fit my desired one. This is what the output looked like after I finished tracing.: </p> <div style="display: flex; justify-content: center; gap: 20px; max-width: 100%;"> <div style="text-align: center; max-width: 500px;"> <img src="DXFfile.png" alt="2D design of grocery bag holder in fusion" style="width: 100%; border-radius: 8px;"> <p style="margin-top: 8px; font-size: 14px; color: #c9c9c9;"> 2D sketch of grocery bag holder </p> </div> </div> <br> <p> Next I copied the dxf file on a drive and uploaded it to the shopbot computer. I opened the file in Aspire and with the help of Bobby did a bit more file processing there. Here is a video of the CNC cutting the uploaded file. </p> <div style="text-align: center; max-width: 300px; margin: 0 auto;"> <video width="100%" height="auto" controls> <source src="CNCatWork.mp4" type="video/mp4"> Your browser does not support the video tag. </video> <p style="margin-top: 8px; font-size: 14px; color: #c9c9c9;">CNC cutting out the handle. Oddly satisfying.</p> </div> <br> <p> This is how the holder looks just out of the CNC. The indmill bit was too big for the size of my name so it is not as clear as I hoped it would be, but I am still happy with the result. The edges are also very sharp and not smooth at all. We will have to do some post processing to make it look better. </p> <div style="display: flex; justify-content: center; gap: 20px; max-width: 100%;"> <div style="text-align: center; max-width: 400px;"> <img src="CompleteProduct.jpg" alt="Cut out Holder" style="width: 100%; border-radius: 8px;"> <p style="margin-top: 8px; font-size: 14px; color: #c9c9c9;"> Cut out holder. </p> </div> </div> <h4 class="title">Post Processing</h4> <p> For post processing, first I tried a machine that curves the edges, but clamping the holder was hard and it got unsafe using the machine.
