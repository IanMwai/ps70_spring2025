For speed calculation, I had already optimized it in week 6 so I just needed to carry the setup forward to week 6 and merge it with the LED designs. <br><br> For the LEDs which will serve as indicator lights, the idea was to connect three red LEDs in parallel each connected to a 100 ohm resistor and all of the LEDs connected to an ESP32 pin. I would then have a repeat of this setup in the other side too so I have both right and left indicators. Then I would connect two butttons, one for each side to an input pin and when the buttons are pressed, the LEDs would light up or go off. <br> <br>I tried implementing this but the pins I innitially chose seem to have had a very low output voltage, the LEDs barely lit up. I therefore changed the setup to use an external power supply and a transistor to control the LEDS. There were no MOSFETS in lab so I tried using a BJT, number 2n3906331. This kind of worked because I could get the LEDs to light up, but controlling them was hard. They could not turn the LEDs off completely, it just dimmed them a bit. <br><br> So I had to get MOSFETS from home, I had IRLZ14. These made it much much better. The LEDs were individually conneceted to the power bus via a 100 ohm resistor and then the cathodes were connected to the drain pin. The gate pin was connected to ESP32 for signal control and the source pin grounded. <br><br> I also added functionality such that when a button is pressed, LEDs would turn on and blink and a message will be shown on the OLED display saying which indicator was on or if both were on.
